Sam Duffy

Artist-Printer

About Sam

Sam Duffy is a letterpress printer, poet, and maker of artist books whose work blends traditional craft with images and text derived from the trans experience. Raised in the Bay Area and educated in English at UC Berkeley, their experience with letterpress began as an undergraduate, where they produced their first edition as a collaborative classroom project. Since then, Sam has worked with the CODEX Foundation first as an intern and later as a collaborator with Peter Koch's Real Lead Saloon.

Their most recent project, Fragments of Spring, uses traditional craft and seasonal imagery to explore trans embodiment and artistic credibility. Debuting at the Berkeley Public Library in April 2025, the project responds to a climate of escalating anti-trans violence through a celebration of hope. Sam's current work continues their exploration of trans survival through craft and form.
